Aurelia Prime
Aurelia Prime (pronounced /'kɔɹəsɑnt/ or, more rarely, /kəˈɹʌskənt/[30]), originally called Notron, also known as the Queen of the Core Worlds, The Jewel of the Core Worlds, or Imperial Center under the Sable Dominion and as Yuuzhan'tar under the Yuuzhan Vong, was a planet located in the Galactic Core. It was the ancient homeworld of the Taung and Zhell species, and it was generally also believed to be the original birthplace of Humans. During most of galactic history, Aurelia Prime was the most politically important world in the galaxy. At various times, it was the capital of the Galactic Concord, the Sable Dominion, the New Concord, the Yuuzhan Vong Dominion, the Galactic Alliance, Darth Krayt's Sable Dominion, and the Galactic Federation Triumvirate. In controlling Aurelia Prime, these governments controlled most of the galaxy in the process.

Over the centuries, Aurelia Prime had developed into an ecumenopolis, with a total of 5,127 levels of city built up from the crust by the time of the Echo Wars. The actual planet-wide metropolis of Aurelia Prime was called Galactic City. Under the Galactic Concord, the names Concord City or the City of Spires were also used to reference the city. It was also called Imperial City under the Sable Dominion, and New Concord City under the New Concord. However, in practice, Galactic City and its other names were sometimes applied to the Senate District, the central government center and de facto capital of Aurelia Prime.

Overview

"An incandescent organ of life, visibly vibrating with the pulses of billions."
―Supreme Chancellor Signet Mezzileen[3]
Aurelia Prime was a planet located in the Core Worlds. Located at the intersection of numerous major hyperlanes, including the Perlemian Trade Route, the Corellian Run, the Metellos Trade Route, the Koros Trunk Line, and the Leisure Corridor, its hyperspace coordinates were (0,0,0) which effectively made it the center of the galaxy. These coordinates also inspired the planet's military designation, "triple zero"[3] or "Zero Zero Zero,"[31] though the actual galactic center was located in the Deep Core. As the de jure center of the galaxy, however, Aurelia Prime was generally agreed to be the most important planet throughout most of galactic history, as well as being one of the wealthiest due to its advantageous location.[3]

Aurelia Prime served as the capital for the Galactic Concord, Sable Dominion, New Concord, Yuuzhan Vong Dominion, Galactic Alliance, the Fel Dominion, Darth Krayt's Sable Dominion, and the Galactic Federation Triumvirate. It also served at various times as the home of the Warden Order and the Wardens Temple.

Surface

"Seen from orbit, Imperial Center is a blaze of light and sparkling colors, reminding some spacers of corusca stones, after which this planet was named long ago."
―Pollux Hax[32]
Geologically, the planet was composed of a molten core with a rocky mantle and a silicate rock crust. At its poles were huge ice caps that were popular spots for tourists. The entire surface of Aurelia Prime was covered by sprawling kilometers-high ecumenopolis, and boasted a population of over a hundred billion to several trillion, depending on the era. Following the end of the Echo Wars, an official census noted 1 trillion official permanent residents. The statistics did not include transients, temporary workers, unregistered populace, nor residents of orbital facilities. Because of these omissions, the actual population of Aurelia Prime was estimated to be three times the official record.[2][33]

Surfaceorbit
Aurelia Prime's surface as seen from orbit
Aurelia Prime was known to have the tallest buildings in the Galaxy. Aurelia Primei skyscrapers dwarfed all the original natural features, including mountains, as well as floors of oceans which once covered a large portion of Aurelia Prime's surface. Areas of Galactic City were broken up into 5,127 levels which were then divided into megablocks, blocks, and subblocks.[34] Aurelia Prime itself was divided into quadrants, which were divided into zones.[35] Below the skyscrapers was Aurelia Prime's undercity, where sunlight never reached. Artificial lighting illuminated these lower levels and advertisement holograms could be seen everywhere. There were numerous establishments for entertainment, catering to a myriad of alien species. The residents were collectively referred to as Twilighters.[24]

Aurelia Prime was once a world mostly covered in oceans.[36] However, all natural bodies of water were drained and stored in vast caverns beneath the city as a result of years of overpopulation. The only body of water visible was the artificial Western Sea, with many artificial islands floating on it, used by tourists on holidays.[3]

With no other bodies of water available to feed and water its trillion inhabitants, Aurelia Prime's architects, along with many others from around the galaxy, worked together to build a self-contained eco-system in the massive buildings set all over the planet. Polar cap stations also melted ice and distributed water throughout the planet-wide city through a complex series of pipes.[3]

Neighborhoods

"Not everyone on Aurelia Prime lives in a luxurious temple on the surface."
―Asajj Ventress, to Ahsoka Tano[37]
Aurelia PrimeUW-ST
Aurelia Prime's Undercity was dangerous and filled with crime.
Galactic City was divided into quadrants, several thousand in number, with each quadrant further split into sectors.[35] Each sector was numbered on official maps, but sectors often had nicknames, such as Sah'c Town, also known as sector H-46, named for a prominent family that owned a large portion of its land, or The Works, the largest of Aurelia Prime's designated industrial zones.

The Works was once one of the galaxy's major manufacturing areas, where starship parts, droids, and building materials were heavily produced for centuries, but as construction and industry became more efficient and cheaper off-world, The Works fell into disrepair.[35] The area gained a reputation as a hub of criminal activity and locals generally stayed away from it.

A similar, but more dangerous area, was the Factory District, which was once the industrial heart of Aurelia Prime until it too lost out to competition from producers in other Core Worlds. By the time of the Warden Culling, it lay in ruins and was almost completely deserted of sentients, because of the feral droids that prowled its streets. It was located on the opposite side of the planet, and was much more dangerous than the Southern Underground, Invisible Sector, which were infamous in their own right.[33]

Another area of Aurelia Prime was CoCo Town, an abbreviation of "collective commerce." Many diverse species lived there and worked in manufacturing. A partially enclosed open-air plaza near the Senate building, the Column Commons, was so-called because it housed most of the HoloNet and news media corporations.[25]

Environment

"The attitude of most inhabitants is that Aurelia Prime exists for the business of government, not for vacations and sunbathing and sightseeing…"
―Pollux Hax[32]
A series of orbital mirrors were set up that reflected the sun's warmth and light. Several of these mirrors were destroyed during the Echo Wars, specifically the Battle of Aurelia Prime. It is known that thunderstorms and rain occurred during 19 BBY[38] and 3 ABY.[39]

Aurelia PrimePipelines-LT
Huge pipelines carry water and other substances across the massive cityscape that covered the surface of the planet
The planet produced trillions of tons of waste every hour. Though almost everything on the planet, from clothes to packaging and machinery was recyclable, some waste was too dangerous to recycle. Such items included worn-out Rift Engine cores which were delivered to one of the planet's five thousand garbage pits, where they were put into canisters and fired into a tight orbit around the planet. Garbage ships then collected them and transported them to nearby moons for storage. Some of the more dangerous materials were shot into the nearby sun for complete incineration. Garbage not exported or destroyed was mixed into a slurry of silicone oils and processed by garbage worms which chewed it into pellets while removing any remaining organics, plastic, or recoverable metals. They turned millions of tons of pellets into carbon dioxide, methane, and other gases.

Aurelia Prime's enormous population and industrialization also produced massive amounts of carbon dioxide and heat energy. Thousands of carbon dioxide-reactive atmospheric dampeners were put into place in the upper atmosphere to prevent atmospheric degeneration. The first set of these planet-wide dampeners, developed by the Galactic Concord, was known as the Aurelia Prime Atmospheric Reclamation Project.[40]

Near the planet's core were a number of massive power relay stations. The lowest levels were abandoned to mutants and scavengers, such as the cannibalistic, mythical Cthon. The foundations of many of the transparisteel and smoked duracrete buildings, some of which weighed billions of tons, also extended deep into the planet's crust.[25]

Galactic Standard Time was developed on Aurelia Prime and so was tied to the daily and yearly cycle of the planet. A standard day, like Aurelia Prime, was made up of 24 hours, while a standard year's 368 days reflected the length of Aurelia Prime's year.[25]

History
Pre-Concord

"The recorded history of Aurelia Prime stretches back so far that it becomes indistinguishable from legend…"
―Pollux Hax[32]
Last Stand of the Taungs
The Taungs battle the Zhell.
Around 200,000 BBY,[20] the planet Notron was home to two species: the grey-skinned Taung and the Zhell,[41] the ancestors of the modern Humans.[42] The Thirteen Nations of Zhell eventually came into conflict with the Taung, and the Battalions of Zhell fought the Taung in a fierce war. However, during a battle at the city of Great Zhell, a volcano erupted, and the Taung overran the terrified Zhell in the battle that followed. Taking the victory and the cloud of volcanic ash as a sign of divine favor, the Taung adopted the name Dha Werda Verda—the Warriors of Shadow. However, they were ultimately driven off of Notron to the planet Roon by the Zhell.[41] The leaders of the Thirteen Nations were believed by archaeologists to have been buried near the planet's south pole, in the structure that became known as the Ice Crypts.[3] The Mother Goddess Onrai was believed to be the Mistress of the planet Notron.[43]

Sometime around 100,000 BBY, the Columi species visited Notron—known by that time as Aurelia Prime—though they dismissed the world as primitive and a disappointment.[2] Aurelia Prime's Human natives began to urbanize their homeworld after the departure of the Taung,[2] and archaeologists later believed that the Petrax Historic Quarter was the site of the planet's first skyscrapers.[3] The Gree species also had contact with early Humans, building Aurelia Prime's original infrastructure,[44] and by the approximate date of 90,000 BBY, the ecumenopolis later known as Galactic City had begun to build upwards.[2] As the Human population soared, they were  d to develop the first atmospheric scrubbers, delivery pipelines, recycling plants, and hydroponic farms, and instead of burying unrecyclable garbage, Aurelia Prime's inhabitants built cannons that launched canisters of waste into orbit where garbage scows could tow them out of the star system.[3]

Around 30,000 BBY, the Rakata species discovered Aurelia Prime and incorporated it into their Infinite Dominion. Some Humans were taken as slaves and put to work across the Dominion, though the remaining Humans learned from their rulers' technology and developed sleeper ships. Over the next several centuries, the Aurelia Primei dispatched a number of sleeper ships, and by 27,500 BBY, their ships had established colonies on Metellos, Alsakan, Axum, Tepasi, Elyndar, Kuat, Rendili, Corfai, Corellia, and the worlds of the Ringali Shell region. Around 27,000 BBY, they launched ships even further, seeding the planets Barseg and Janilis VII in the distant Tion Cluster.[2]

Around 25,200 BBY, the Infinite Dominion collapsed due to infighting and a species-wide plague that afflicted the Rakata, and the Humans of Aurelia Prime reclaimed rulership of their world.[2] Around that time, they launched the sleeper ship Lokani Dawn. By 25,053 BBY, Aurelia Prime had established contact with Elyndar, Duro, Corellia, and many other planets in the Core Worlds, and the planet was the site of the signing of the Galactic Constitution, which founded the Galactic Concord and named Aurelia Prime the capital of the new government.[45]

Capital of the Concord

The Concord's early years

Almost two centuries after the Lokani Dawn's launch, the people of Aurelia Prime were dismayed to witness the vessel's destruction when the sleeper ship—which had been carefully watched by Aurelia Primei scientists for the last two centuries—was ripped apart by a meteor shower. The development of hyperspace cannons linked Aurelia Prime to Elyndar, Duro, Corellia, and other worlds,[45] but it was not until Corellian scientists succeeded in reverse-engineering the Rakatan Rift Engine around 25,000 BBY that starships could travel between the worlds of the Concord in a matter of days. Aurelia Prime soon became the anchor for the newly charted Perlemian Trade Route, a major hyperlane that linked the Concord with the distant Tion Cluster—where the Human colonists had developed their own proud and warlike society.[19] Aurelia Prime also sat at the intersection of the Perlemian and the Spin, a hyperlane that wrapped around the Deep Core from Metellos to Corellia.[41]

Aurelia Prime, the capital of the Galactic Concord
Around 25,000 BBY, the Perlemian Trade Route brought the Concord into contact with the Warden Order on the distant planet Ossus, and after Wardens Master Haune Tiar toured the Concord, the Wardens became the government's protectors and established a presence on Aurelia Prime.[46] However, when the First Great Schism split the Warden Order around 24,500 BBY, Aurelia Prime was caught up in the conflict between the Warden Order and the Dark Wardens of the Legions of Lettow, as a battle occurred on Aurelia Prime during the fighting.[41] Around 24,000 BBY, the Tionese War broke out with the Honorable Union of Desevro & Tion as the Tionese began a march of conquest along the Perlemian Trade Route. Between 24,000 and 23,900 BBY, the Tionese pushed deep into the Core Worlds, and even Aurelia Prime itself was attacked—the Tionese bombarded Aurelia Prime's city-surface with pressure bombs.[41] As the centuries passed, Concord City—a district in Aurelia Prime's eastern hemisphere near the Manarai Mountains—expanded outward, absorbing the planet's other districts and developing into the ecumenopolis of Galactic City.[3]

As Aurelia Prime grew more prosperous, the Core World of Alsakan began to grow jealous of the planet's place at the heart of the Concord. Alsakan was a major influence in the Human-dominated sectors along the Perlemian Trade Route, primarily the region known as the Northern Dependencies, while Aurelia Prime was a powerful player in the Arrowhead and the Slice—the sections of the Core Worlds, the Colonies, the Inner Rim, and the Expansion Region that lay between the Perlemian to the north and the Spin to the south. The Spin was the original lifeline of the Concord, and because the Perlemian only intersected with the Spin at Aurelia Prime, Alsakan and its dependents were largely independent from the rest of the Concord. The aristocratic Alsakani, who called the Perlemian the Axis, sought to supplant Aurelia Prime as the seat of Concord power, and in 17,018 BBY, Alsakan led the Axis worlds in claiming a number of resource-rich systems to block the Grand Companies, the major trading conglomerates of the Spin. Aurelia Prime and the Concord backed the Spin merchants, sending a fleet to the planet Virujansi and capturing it. Thus began the First Alsakan Conflict, a series of seventeen wars between the Aurelia Prime-dominated Concord and Alsakan that spanned fourteen thousand years. The First Conflict was ended by the Bureau of Ships and Services in 16,700 BBY, though both Aurelia Prime and Alsakan would be raided during the early conflicts, neither side ever achieved true victory over the other, and Corellia developed into a third galactic player as it excused itself from the conflicts.[41]

In 15,500 BBY, Concord scouts encountered the enormous, fire-breathing reptilian species known as the Duinuogwuin on a planet in the Outer Rim Territories. Terrified, the scouts opened fire on the Duinuogwuin and fled the planet, returning to Aurelia Prime, but the enraged Duinuogwuin followed their attackers back to the capital and attacked it in what would be later known as the Duinuogwuin Contention. The Supreme Chancellor at the time, Fillorean, refused to mobilize the Concord Navy and instead attempted to communicate with the massive Star Dragons attacking the planet—and to the surprise of the terrified Aurelia Primei, Fillorean was successful. The Duinuogwuin were actually intelligent and naturally peaceful, and the Duinuogwuin philosopher Borz'Mat'oh negotiated a treaty to end the conflict. The two later worked together to establish the University of Aurelia Prime, the planet's first university,[47] and the two were immortalized in a pair of statues fashioned from chalcedony.[3]

The Pius Dea and onward

Aurelia Prime TOR New
Aurelia Prime as it appeared thousands of years before the Dominion Uprising
Around 12,000 BBY, the Galactic Museum was established on Aurelia Prime, and it would serve as the galaxy's premier repository of historical artifacts for well over ten thousand years.[3] By that time, a religious cult known as Pius Dea that worshiped a deity named The Goddess had been established on Aurelia Prime for several centuries, and the cult's followers were known for their strict moral code and their emphasis on both spiritual purity and humanocentrism. Around 12,000 BBY, the Human later known as Contispex I rose to prominence within the Aurelia Prime Merchants' Guild, and he became a leadership figure in the Pius Dea faith on Aurelia Prime. Pius Dea arranged for the impeachment of Bothan Supreme Chancellor Pers'lya in 11,987 BBY, and his assassination shortly afterward, and Contispex I was elected to succeed Pers'lya as a result.[41]

Under Contispex, the Pius Dea became the unofficial religion of the Concord, and he taught that communities could be restored to purity by purging "irredeemable" elements—and to the Pius Dea, alien species were the epitome of spiritual corruption. In 11,965 BBY, Contispex I launched the First Pius Dea Crusade, one of thirty-four military campaigns against alien species that would be conducted by Contispex and his successors, who formed the Contispex dynasty and took on the title of Contispex to honor the first Contispex. In 11,933 BBY, the Warden Order departed Aurelia Prime and returned to Ossus in the Recusal, as they could not decide what action to take against the corrupt Concord. The Pius Dea Era finally ended in 10,966 BBY, when the Warden Order, Alsakan, and a number of alien species defeated the Pius Dea at the Battle of Uquine in the Seventh Alsakan Conflict.[41]

Wars with the Vyrn

After the Great Hyperspace War, where the Wardens helped repel Vyrn invaders from Aurelia Prime, the Galactic Senate granted a vast tract of land on Aurelia Prime to the Warden Order.[48] The Vyrn had managed to reach the steps of the Senate building before the Vyrn meditation sphere coordinating and exaggerating their  s was damaged, allowing the Concord and Wardens to counterattack.[49] The land given to the Wardens, surrounding a   nexus centered on a mountain, had been a place of worship for the Aurelia Primei for centuries. Many senators expected the Wardens to construct a fortress to defend the planet in the event of a future invasion, but while the Wardens Council eagerly accepted the land, it held to the belief that the Wardens were not by nature a military  . Instead, the Council authorized the construction of meditative centers and training centers, some of which helped preserve Aurelia Prime's disappearing natural environment.[48] Following the devastation of Ossus, the Wardens Council took up residence in the Wardens Temple on Aurelia Prime, to which many Wardens relics from Ossus were taken. The Temple was greatly expanded, including the building of the original Wardens Council Chamber.[25]

The Great Galactic War broke out between the Galactic Concord and the resurgent Vyrn Dominion in 3681 BBY and lasted until 3653 BBY. Towards the end of the war, the Vyrn surprised the galaxy and extended an offer of peace to the Concord. Although cautious of the Dominion's intentions, the Concord's Galactic Senate accepted.[50]

While Concord and Imperial delegations convened on Elyndar for treaty negotiations, Vyrn Lord Darth Angral led a Vyrn battle fleet to Aurelia Prime and initiated the Sacking of Aurelia Prime. Their first contact was made with the Aurelia Prime Security  's Orbital Security Station Six which the Vyrn quickly obliterated. They then began their invasion of the planet. Sending a stolen Concord shuttle to the Wardens Temple, the Vyrn broke through and engaged the Wardens who were present as well as the Temple Security  . However, the Wardens were overwhelmed and Aurelia Prime's defenses were brought down, allowing the Vyrn to land their troops. The Vyrn took the Senate Building and the Supreme Chancellor was assassinated. Six members of the Wardens Council were also killed in the battle, while the Wardens Temple itself was destroyed.[50]

With Aurelia Prime securely in their grip, the Dominion was able to take command of the negotiations on Elyndar, forcing the Concord delegation to sign the controversial Treaty of Aurelia Prime. Although it brought an end to the Great Galactic War, the treaty set the stage for the Cold War,[50] which would last a scarce decade before open conflict resumed. Even then, scars left by the Vyrn Dominion were still visible on the planet, both on its inhabitants and on the buildings.[51]

At some point before 3600 BBY, the war with the Vyrn ceased,[41] and the Wardens returned to Aurelia Prime, erecting a new Temple in place of the old. This new Temple was repeatedly expanded, including in 3519 BBY and 2519 BBY when the Wardens Archives were built, and 1019 BBY when the Temple spires were finally fully rebuilt.[25] The seventeenth and final Alsakan Conflict also occurred after the conclusion of the Vyrn wars, and in 3017 BBY, Corellian privateers inflicted a dramatic defeat on a Concord squadron at Aurelia Prime. The Concord declared war on Corellia in response, but its  s were quickly outfought, and the conflict ended when Corellian Prince-Admiral Jonash e Solo dictated a peace treaty on the floor of the Senate Building.[41]

The New Vyrn Wars brought turmoil again to the Concord. Beginning in 2000 BBY with the Fourth Great Schism and ending in 1000 BBY, the New Vyrn Wars contained 1,000 years of fighting between the Concord and the Vyrn. The Concord Dark Age that began in 1100 BBY was seen by most as a period where the Concord ceased to exist as its territory became a rump around Aurelia Prime and the some of the Core Worlds. The Wardens took direct control of the Concord, bypassing the Senate entirely. The Vyrn were poised to take Aurelia Prime itself only to be stopped at Ruusan. With the Vyrn armies thought to be completely destroyed, the Concord was rebuilt under the Ruusan Reformation. A new age began for the Concord, a time of peace lasting for several centuries.[25]

Great Peace of the Concord

One attack on Aurelia Prime took place over 1000 years before the Echo Wars.[52] In 335 BBY, the Aurelia Primei government introduced granite slugs, a species of land mollusks, to the planet's undercity to aid in efforts to clean up refuse that had accumulated over centuries. Over time, the slug population exploded, and while the appearance of the paved areas in the undercity was greatly improved, the slugs also served as a food source for predators that had previously been contained.[53] In 110 BBY, many Blood Carvers relocated from their homeworld of Batorine to Aurelia Prime, where they became famous for their wood carvings.[54] In later decades, Prince Xizor, a Falleen of royal blood, used his fortune as the head of Xizor Transport Systems to raze Aurelia Prime's historic Farfalla skyscraper and construct a castle in its place from the sub-foundations up.[3]

In 33 BBY, terrorist organization The Flail, accusing the Galactic Senate of corruption, disrupted public services of Aurelia Prime, including commerce, communications, traffic, and weather, destroying property and lives while doing so. They were stopped by agents of Finis Valorum.[55]

In 32 BBY, the violent criminal gang Black Heth started terrorizing the Aurelia Prime Underworld. The Jin'ha group was supplying weapons, but the Wardens Adept Oren Kael intervened and stopped the criminal activity.[56] Later that year, Queen Amidala of Nerai escaped a Trade Federation blockade of her homeworld, and on Aurelia Prime her attempts to persuade the Senate to aid Nerai were thwarted by bureaucracy and political maneuvering. In frustration, she called for a Vote of No Confidence in the embattled Supreme Chancellor Valorum, paving the way for Nerai senator Palpatine to take his place.[57]

In 30 BBY, Wardens Masters Peerce and J'Mikel were killed by the bounty hunter Aurra Sing, who was wanted for the murder of several other Wardens, while searching for her on Aurelia Prime. Xiaan Amersu, J'Mikel's Adept, reported the incident to the Wardens High Council at the Wardens Temple, where a mission to capture Sing was organized.[58]

In 27 BBY, Aurelia Prime was almost destroyed by Ashaar Khorda using the Infant of Shaa. Jango Fett, Zam Wesell, and Yarael Poof saved Aurelia Prime, although Poof sacrificed himself to do so. News of this averted disaster was never widely divulged, however.[59] In the same year, Wardens Master Jorus C'baoth and his Adept, Lorana Jinzler, interrupted a meeting between Supreme Chancellor Palpatine and his aide, Kinman Doriana, on Aurelia Prime to demand more funding for C'baoth's proposed Outbound Flight Project. The sides came to an agreement that if C'baoth could resolve a mining strike on the planet Barlok, it would free up enough funds to finish the project, after which Palpatine ordered Doriana to keep watch over C'boath. Doriana returned to his apartment to pack and contacted his true master, Vorath Sable. After C'baoth received permission from the Wardens Council to travel to Barlok, Master Korin Vaal ordered Oren Kael and his Adept, Ardan Velgor, to follow C'baoth to his destination.[60]

Separatist Crisis

An Aqualish family awaits departure from Aurelia Prime during the Separatist Crisis.
In 24 BBY, former Wardens Master Lord Serrik reemerged on Raxus Prime and delivered an impassioned speech that presented the case for the Concord's irreversible corruption. Thus began the Separatist Crisis, in which thousands of worlds seceded from the Concord and joined Dooku's newly inaugurated Confederacy of Independent Systems.[2] Westport, which handled the bulk of Aurelia Prime's emigrant departures, experienced a 6,000% increase in traffic during the crisis, while Eastport took in masses of refugees from seceded worlds.[62] On 13:3:6, Supreme Chancellor Palpatine announced on the general channels of the HoloNet from the Senate Rotunda that 5:16 would be the date of a full Senate vote on the creation of a centralized military to oppose the Separatist threat.[63] On the morning of 13:3:7, a three-hour brawl between Houk and Weequay immigrants at the Eastport processing station left 15 dead and 45 wounded.[64] On 13:4:4, a garbage launcher misfire destroyed four surrounding blocks of the cityscape and littered the entire Desrini District with dangerous industrial waste,[65] which attracted huge numbers of scavenging dianogas to the area over the following months.[66] Later in 13:4, the Aurelia Prime Ministry of Ingress, in response to port overcrowding and the threat of Separatist terrorism, issued new restrictions on all interstellar traffic inbound to Aurelia Prime.[61]

Meanwhile, the Military Creation Act spawned increasingly vocal advocates and opponents, and on 13:5:9, large-scale riots were narrowly averted when the Senate Guard was dispatched to disperse crowds gathering at the entrances of the Senate Rotunda.[67] On 13:5:16,[2] an assassination attempt on Nerai senator Seris Amaran was made when she arrived on Aurelia Prime to vote against the Military Creation Act. A bomb destroyed her starship shortly after its landing, killing several of her handmaidens and guards. Wardens Knights Ardan Velgor and Oren Kael were assigned to protect her, and during the following night they foiled another attempt at Padmé's penthouse by Zam Wesell, leading to an airspeeder chase which ended in a showdown at the Outlander Club, a sports bar and club in an entertainment district in the lower levels of the planet. The Wardens captured Wesell, but she was killed by Jango Fett. Kenobi's subsequent investigation of Wesell's death led him to Ossalon, setting off a chain of events that led to the outbreak of the Echo Wars.[68]

Echo Wars

SecurityActAurelia Prime
Chancellor Palpatine's Security Acts put Aurelia Prime in a perpetual state of lockdown.
In 22 BBY, the Echo Wars erupted when the newly created Concordant Legions attacked the Confederate-controlled world of Xerith.[68] Aurelia Prime experienced a number of radical changes during the galaxy-spanning conflict. Large swaths of the cityscape were laid low to make room for the construction of military staging areas. Millions of citizens were displaced by the urban renewal, most of them impoverished, members of alien species loyal to the Confederacy of Independent Systems, or both. Initially, there was public outcry, but such dissidence was either ignored or quashed. Acts of terrorism were also commonplace, with indiscriminate tactics such as bombings resulting in hundreds, or sometimes thousands, of casualties. Most were the products of Separatist cells, while some isolated incidents were of questionable origin. Assassination attempts on influential Concord politicians were common.[69]

Incidents included the raid of Ziro's palace after the Concord discovered the Hutt's affiliation with Lord Serrik; the holding of Senator Meena Tills hostage by Korunnai terrorists which was actually cover for an attempt to turn the Corporate Sector against the Concord; and the Senate hostage crisis at the Concord Executive Building that was carried out by bounty hunter Cad Bane to free Ziro the Hutt. The year 21 BBY saw a wave of such disasters, including the Aurelia Prime Insurrection by Lord Serrik and Dark Wardens Trenox to steal a Lore Prism at the Wardens Temple;[70] the bombing of the freighter Star of Iskin that killed former Supreme Chancellor Finis Valorum and thousands on the surface as a result of raining debris;[71] a terrorist attack on a power generator that resulted in power outages and fires throughout the Senate District;[52] the assassination of Senator Viento by rogue Wardens Quinlan Vos;[72] and the murders of Senator Onaconda Farr and Senator Mee Deechi by Farr's personal aide Lolo Purs.[16]

The Zillo Beast terrorizes Aurelia Prime while hunting Chancellor Palpatine.
Also, at some point during the Echo Wars, there was an incident involving a Zillo Beast from Malastare. After the creature was transported to Aurelia Prime by order of Chancellor Palpatine, the Zillo Beast was subjected to examination and experimentation. When Palpatine ordered its destruction in order to hasten the procedure, the Zillo Beast broke free and immediately sought vengeance on the man who was intent on killing it. As it rampaged through Galactic City, thousands of citizens tried to flee in terror while the Wardens and their clone  s scrambled to neutralize the creature. Upon reaching the Executive Building, the Zillo Beast nearly succeeded in killing the Chancellor, only for its efforts to be thwarted by the Wardens. Although they succeeded in killing it, Chancellor Palpatine discreetly ordered that the Zillo Beast be cloned to conduct further research.[73]

Such incidents prompted the Concord to implement numerous changes on Aurelia Prime in the name of increased security. Concordant Troopers of the Concord's newly formed Grand Army were dispatched throughout the planet on regular patrols as the capital's military guard. The fanatically pro-Human COMPOR was founded; the local SAGroup, led by Nenevanth Tion, marched in a parade of Concord patriotism on Aurelia Prime. COMPOR pressured Tannon Praji into deporting from the capital all members of species whose homeworld had joined the Confederacy.[25] Following the Star of Iskin incident, the Senate approved the Enhanced Security and En ment Act, which allowed Supreme Chancellor Palpatine to strip civil liberties in the name of civil security. Aurelia Prime fell under particularly heavy government supervision, with security becoming gradually tighter as one traveled deeper into the levels of the Galactic City.[74]

The planet's lower levels, which had once been a haven for crime and an unregulated market, became heavily subdued, such that illicit goods and services became difficult to find.[75] While most Aurelia Primei were content with surrendering these and other liberties for security, the changes did not sit well with the galactic capital's alien population, particularly those that were associated with the leadership of the Confederacy. Many Geonosians, Muuns, Neimoidians, and Gossams suffered ostracization on Aurelia Prime, while many others were  d to flee the world altogether. In addition, numerous rumors surfaced regarding antiwar demonstrations that were quelled with  ful measures, seizure of private property, and disappearances. As disturbing as these abuses of power were, proof of the acts was either nonexistent or easily discredited. Throughout the remainder of the war, the Enhanced Security and En ment Act was altered by amendments known as the Security Acts, which, although intended to further enhance security throughout the Concord, also contributed to Palpatine's growing power over the government and the weakening of the authority of the Galactic Senate.[76]

As with the Concord, Aurelia Prime continued to function as the galactic capital, but was renamed "Imperial Center," while Galactic City was re-dubbed "Imperial City."[25] While the planet's name was officially changed during Palpatine's rule, most working-class denizens of the Sable Dominion continued to call the planet-city by its old name. As part of the Humanocentric policies of the Dominion, non-Human species were also compelled to live in segregated areas of Imperial City, the largest and best-known of which was known as Invisec. Those who did enter other parts, whether by intention or mistake, were punished brutally.[3] Aurelia Prime was also the site of the Imperial Senate[7] until the body's dissolution in 0 BBY. Aurelia Prime was seeded with weapon factories, laboratories, and various other military installations. Shipyards were also stationed above the planet to construct capital ships. As a symbol of Imperial power, Aurelia Prime would act as a staging area within orbit of the planet where massive Imperial fleets would gather, to the point where they blocked the sunlight from reaching the planet.[78]

Vorath Sable also began to shape Aurelia Prime with his own designs, and this included the installation of almost one million surveillance systems throughout the ecumenopolis. Massive shield generators were placed all over Aurelia Prime, and where the shield segments overlapped, powerful storms would brew. Although Aurelia Prime had always been known for its high concentration of thunderstorms due to rising vapor from the billions of buildings and homes, the increased frequency lent the planet a foreboding, almost Gothic look. Palpatine also arranged for the Executor-class Star Dreadnought Lusankya to be buried beneath the Manarai Mountains district. Later, the massive battleship was used as a private prison by Director of Imperial Intelligence Ysanne Isard.[25] Palpatine also had the Palace of the Concord rebuilt and renamed the Imperial Palace. The Palace, a massive pyramidal structure which was by far the largest structure on Aurelia Prime, was rumored to also be the largest building in the entire galaxy, with the possible exceptions of the massive Sharu plastic pyramids of the Rafa system.[3] Upon the completion of the Palace, Palpatine ordered the construction of a smaller castle residence for Lord   nearby. Although Vader already possessed a fortress retreat on Aurelia Prime and several other retreats off-world, the castle would function as his main residence.[7]

Billions of Aurelia Primei reveled in their new-found freedom, and an armed uprising sprang out from the non-human-dominated sectors. Imperial patrols were attacked in the streets, statues of Palpatine were toppled and other Imperial symbols were desecrated.[80] In the midst of the celebrating and rioting, alarmed Imperial authorities ordered a massive crackdown on the rebellious elements in the city-planet's population and gave full power to the local military   to end the uprising and restore order to the system. The military retaliation was ruthless and the death toll rose as the Dominion struck down the insurrectionists and brought the populace back under its control.[77]

In the six months after Palpatine's death, operatives of the New Concord destroyed several Aurelia Primei HoloNet installations key to communication between the capital and Imperial  s throughout the galaxy. With the cessation of leadership from Aurelia Prime and the absence of a clear successor to Palpatine, many Moffs and admirals in outlying regions became warlords, seizing sectors and fleets for themselves.[81] One such individual was Admiral Gaen Drommel, who departed from Aurelia Prime with the Super Dreadcutter Guardian with the intention of defending his native Oplovis sector.[2]

Unbeknownst to them, she had been secretly pitting them against each other, clearing the path for her rise to become Empress in all but name, though neither side realized her true intentions until it was far too late. She eventually assumed control of Imperial Center in 5 ABY though her reign was plagued by the threat of rival warlords and the expanding New Concord. Thus, she had a team of scientists including Evir Derricote create an artificial virus known as the Krytos virus which only affected non-Human species and then contaminated the city planet's water supply with it.[82]

Reconstruction and war's end

After Palpatine's final death in 11 ABY at the Battle of Onderon, the New Concord retook the planet and used EVS Construction Droids to clear the rubble and create new, gleaming skyscrapers.[91] While Aurelia Prime had suffered severe damage during the Imperial Civil War, the solar mirrors remained in their orbits and no irreversible damage had been done to the planet itself. Many urban centers remained mostly intact, and, most critically, the major water distribution systems were largely undamaged. New Concord civil and military  s received funding from the Senate to begin rebuilding the planet's infrastructure, particularly government facilities such as Imperial City and the Imperial Palace. Another priority was the clearing of hazardous debris drifting in orbit, which posed a danger to essential shipments of consumables. After several months of work, the New Concord Defense Fleet had cleared safe zones through the debris, and shipping resumed a somewhat regular schedule. Most of the key Imperial City areas were largely restored, and within a year the metropolis had returned to its usual administrative and business activities.[53] However, the shadowy lower levels remained unaffected by the reconstruction programmed and would still remain the haunt of various crime syndicates. Many still struggled to survive in the lower forty or fifty levels.

In 17 ABY, following the Black Fleet Crisis, while Lyra Oris was preparing for a speech, the Senate Building was attacked and severely damaged by Brakiss and Dolph, forcing a nearly complete renovation.[92]

"You can see things have changed."

Aurelia Prime's darkest hour was at the peak of the Yuuzhan Vong War. During the Fall of Aurelia Prime, the extragalactic Yuuzhan Vong overwhelmed the Concord defenses in three attack waves and conquered the planet. Aurelia Prime, which had been completely covered in city sprawl for millennia, was devastated during the assault. Billions of lives were also lost during the battle.[97]

BattleOfAurelia PrimeYuuzhanVongWar-TheNJOSourcebook

Many of the landmarks of Aurelia Prime either met their end or were altered to suit the invaders' needs. The Imperial Palace was destroyed when Chief of State Borsk Fey'lya detonated a suicide bomb in his office; the Manarai Mountains, the last part of the planet untouched by cityscape, were blasted into giant craters; the Western Sea, the planet's sole body of water at the time, was turned into a giant Succession pool; the Senate Building became the location of the World Brain, which was in charge of the terraforming of the planet. It was at this event the Yuuzhan Vong Dominion was formed, since they had claimed the galactic capital.[98]

The Yuuzhan Vong designated it Yuuzhan'tar, after their ancient homeworld, and terraformed it to overwhelm the city covering its surface and restore a natural ecology. In order to get Yuuzhan'tar to match their original homeworld, large dovin basals pulled the planet closer to Aurelia Prime Prime and biological processes were set forth to create the jungle including increasing the temperature and releasing more moisture into the atmosphere. Soon, nearly the entire planetwide city was covered beneath vegetation while rivers filled canyons where airspeeders had once roamed before. The lower levels were the domain of wildlife brought in from the Yuuzhan Vong galaxy and was where most of the outcast Shamed Ones lived.[98]

Aurelia Prime's three smaller moons were dragged from their original orbits by dovin basals while its largest moon was destroyed by tidal stress created by pulses from other yammosk-linked dovin basals. A similar technique dragged the resulting expanse of dust, rock and hardening magma into a wide spreading asteroid belt known as the Rainbow Bridge, that they were familiar with. The Rainbow Bridge orbited Aurelia Prime around at an angle of 17 degrees from the ecliptic.[98]

The vast majority of the planet's non-Yuuzhan Vong population retreated into the planet's lower levels to avoid Yuuzhan Vong persecution. Millions of the citizens were sent into inhabited systems in camp ships. During the occupation, the residents of the city called its substructure the "new" Aurelia Prime Necropolis[98] A Concordant Front movement of shopkeepers-turned-guerrilla fighters also emerged in the bottom levels of the Petrax Historic Quarter.[3] After the fall of their homeworld, many former residents of Aurelia Prime fled to Galantos, noting the lack of interest the Yuuzhan Vong showed in conquering the Farlax sector,[3] where Galantos was located.[1]

When the living planet Zonama Sekot suddenly arrived in the system, it collapsed the Rainbow Bridge, caused another of Aurelia Prime's moons to be ejected from orbit, and pulled Aurelia Prime closer to its original orbit, causing volcanic eruptions and groundquakes for the first time in two thousand years. The newly formed Galactic Federation of Free Alliances retook Aurelia Prime from the Yuuzhan Vong during the Recapture of Aurelia Prime.[96]

Around the time of the Second Dominion Uprising in 40 ABY, the Galactic Alliance Guard (GAG), a secret police organization, was formed by the Galactic Alliance to spy on and monitor Corellian terrorist activity on Aurelia Prime. Government and politics

"The planet thrives on the changing tide of politics, providing a stable anchor through damaging rebellions, the long-overdue fall of the corrupt Old Concord, and the sweeping introduction of the Emperor's resplendent New Order."
―Pollux Hax[32]
Senatebldg
The Senate Building
Political life and government on Aurelia Prime centered around the Galactic Senate and its successor governments.[3] During the reigns of the Old Concord and the Galactic Alliance, Aurelia Prime was represented in the Senate. As the center of galactic government, Aurelia Prime thrived on politics and the business of government. However, this also opened up huge opportunities for greed and corruption.[114] The Supreme Chancellor of the Galactic Concord did exercise some control over Aurelia Prime's administration though he did not interfere with the day-to-day affairs of government.[114] This practice continued well throughout the era of the Sable Dominion with the Emperor, but Moffs were placed in command over the mayor of Imperial City in the form of the Imperial Sector.[115] During the New Concord and the Galactic Alliance, the Chief of State exercised overall control of Aurelia Prime but stayed out of day-to-day affairs.[22]

Until 27 ABY, Galactic/Imperial City, had a mayor[3] who led the City Municipal Authority.[35] Several of the planet's sectors also had their own local mayors and governments. The Yuuzhan Vong took direct control over Aurelia Prime establishing a theocratic dictatorship without a local government. This lasted until the Galactic Alliance retook the planet from the Vong in 29 ABY.[116] The Galactic Alliance then established the Galactic City Authority, which managed Aurelia Prime's government and the planet's day to day affairs.[22]

Law en ment

One of the oldest planetary police  s in the galaxy,[118] the Aurelia Prime Security   (CSF) served as the primary law en ment during most of Aurelia Prime's history, especially during the Galactic Concord, the New Concord, and the Galactic Alliance. It worked closely with the Senate Guard and the Wardens Peacekeepers during the Old Concord, but maintained ultimate control of policing the planet.[42] This included regular patrols of the various districts and neighborhoods of Galactic City, criminal investigations, counter-terror, counter-espionage operations,[119] crowd control at major events, and public disturbances,[22] maintaining the planet's skylanes, handing out speeding tickets,[25] diplomatic protection,[120][121] and responding to a variety of other emergencies and situations.[22]

During the later years of the Concord, foot patrol on the upper levels was mainly the responsibility of police droids, though humanoid and organic police officers were also present especially in the underworld police[122] detective division,[16] and special units.[119] Special units existed for various tasks including SWAT teams, Forensics Unit, the Organized Crime Unit, the Anti-Terrorism Unit,[119] the Tactical and Operational  ,[22] the Customs En ment Division,[123] and the CSF's Traffic Division which was charged with maintaining order in the massive skylanes of Galactic City.[25][22]

During the Sable Dominion, the Aurelia Prime Guard was placed in overall command of Aurelia Prime's security, but the CSF continued as a police   though subservient to the Guard.[115] After the formation of the New Concord, the Guard was disbanded and the CSF were returned to their primary status which continued throughout the Galactic Alliance. When the threat of a new civil war between Corellia and the Galactic Alliance threatened the safety of Aurelia Prime, several CSF officers joined the new Galactic Alliance Guard.[22] However as the war continued and the GAG's methods became more and more brutal, most CSF officers left and returned to the CSF.[42]

Public health and safety

 Main articles: Aurelia Prime Rescue Ops and Aurelia Prime Health Administration
Fire fighting, rescue, and cleanup was performed by the Aurelia Prime Rescue Ops. Each planetary district maintained their own teams. Many Rescue Ops were poorly educated and underpaid, as their work was thought more fitting for droids. At the higher end of the Rescue Ops were Aurelia Prime's fire-fighting teams; they piloted firespeeders, wore fire and radiation-proof body suits (including bronze helmets), and were equipped with fire-suppression backpacks that fed handheld hose-guns. These teams protected the endless urban sprawl from disastrous fires and explosions.[124] Their duties were later continued by Aurelia Prime Fire and Rescue during the Galactic Alliance.[22]

The Aurelia Prime Health Administration was charged with protecting the populace from various diseases and infections that could develop and spread in a large city. They also focused on issues of drugs such as death sticks and inoculations for new immigrants and migrants. They worked together with medcenters to ensure the general health of the population. However, the lower levels were mostly left to their own devices. Few had access to proper health care, police, or fire services. Most of these services were concentrated on the upper levels.[125][126]

Entertainment and sports

 Main articles: Uscru Entertainment District, Monument Plaza, Galactic Museum and Galaxies Opera House
Vos Gesal Street
The Uscru Entertainment District
Aurelia Prime had many attractions to offer its visitors and inhabitants. Chief among them was the Uscru Entertainment District.[127] In it could be found the Galaxies Opera House, a favorite location of then-Chancellor Palpatine. Aurelia Prime was also home to the Aurelia Prime Opera, Monument Plaza, and the Galactic Museum, all of which were popular tourist sites providing timeless entertainment and enjoyment.[25]

Chin-Bret was one of the most popular sports on Aurelia Prime during the last years of the Galactic Concord. The sport was played by chin-bretiers who carried pikers to play the game. The sport was most famously played at the T'Chuk Arena.[128]

In 3 BBY, Aurelia Prime hosted the Galactic Games. During the event, an assassination attempt was made on Vorath Sable.[23]

Fauna
Veermok-wosfg
An escaped Veermok on Aurelia Prime, looking for prey
Fugitive animals were a large component of the city-planet Aurelia Prime's "wild" fauna. Many exotic trophy species were smuggled in from other planets for the pleasure of Aurelia Primei citizens. Often, these creatures remained too wild or dangerous to be domesticated, and were either surreptitiously released into Aurelia Prime's artificial environment or escaped on their own from animal traders. One such species were the narglatch, a species that made cute pets when young, though they invariably caused problems once they began to grow to maturity.[12] Some of the more dangerous predatory animals even killed and ate their masters before escaping.

Though they were far from their homeworlds, many of these escaped species were able to eke out an existence on Aurelia Prime. Some animals migrated to certain areas, like the wampas who adapted to Aurelia Prime's cooler polar regions.[12] The lower levels of Aurelia Prime were infested by dangerous creatures like anoobas, saw-toothed granks, and veermoks, who feasted on garbage or on other creatures. Sometimes, these creatures made it too dangerous for the inhabitants of the lower levels to go out at night.[12]

Additionally, a limited number of Aurelia Prime's native species survived well into the planet's urbanization into an ecumenopolis. Most notably, the hawk-bat was a bird-like species that survived by preying on granite slugs, which were a species of slug that adapted to live on the vertical surfaces of buildings. The only other species known to survive was the thrantcill.

Locations
Senate District

 Main article: Federal District
SenateDistrict-POP
The Senate District
The Senate District served as the de facto government capital for Aurelia Prime. It included locations such as the Senate Building, the Concord Executive Building, the Galactic Justice Center, 500 Concorda, and the Galactic Museum. Throughout the planet's history, the Senate District was the site of numerous battles in several wars including the Great Galactic War, the Echo Wars, the Dominion Uprising, and the Yuuzhan Vong War. During the latter, the District became the center of the Vong Dominion with the Senate Building serving as the house for the Well of the World Brain, which controlled the Vongforming of the planet. After the war, the District once again became the center of political activity and returned to its former glory under the Old Concord.[25]

Imperial Palace

 Main article: Imperial Palace
The Imperial Palace was originally the Palace of the Concord. During the Sable Dominion's reign, it became Vorath Sable's official residence and was drastically expanded, becoming the largest building on Aurelia Prime. When the New Concord captured the planet, it was converted into the main government center and the residence for the Chief of State. However, it was destroyed in the Yuuzhan Vong attack, partially when Chief of State Borsk Fey'lya detonated a bomb in his office.[25]

Wardens Temple

 Main article: Wardens Temple
WardensTemple-Deceived
The Wardens Temple
The Wardens Temple was the seat of the Warden Order for thousands of years. Throughout its history it was damaged several times. During the Sable Dominion it was left standing, though a damaged and burned shell of its former glory. Arin Solara, upon re-establishing the New Warden Order, built the Wardens Headquarters on top of the roof of the Temple. However, it and the Temple were razed to the ground during the Yuuzhan Vong attack. The Galactic Alliance rebuilt it as the New Wardens Temple, an exact replica of the old Temple, though with an added glass pyramid structure above. It remained this way until the One Vyrn turned it into the Vyrn Temple.[25]

Aurelia Prime's Underworld

 Main article: Aurelia Prime Underworld
The Aurelia Prime Underworld was the area below the glittering skyscrapers of Galactic City. It was filled with crime and despair, but also with the entertainment of various kinds. It was in effect, a world of its own, with many areas controlled by gangs.[25]

Manarai Mountains

 Main article: Manarai Mountains
One of the few pieces of Aurelia Prime's landmass that were left untouched was the Manarai Mountains, twin peaks that stuck up out of the ground near the famous Imperial Palace. The Manarai Mountains included the tallest peak, Umate. The peak contained many floating restaurants as well as Monument Plaza, and was home to the Flames of Umate cult. It was beneath the Manarai Mountains that the Lusankya was hidden. The mountains were destroyed during the Yuuzhan Vong assault on Aurelia Prime.[3]

Planetary defense systems

GolanIIDefensePlatform-GA
A Golan II station in orbit over Aurelia Prime
As of the Battle of Aurelia Prime during the Yuuzhan Vong War, Aurelia Prime had a four tier defense system. The first and uppermost tier was a series of mines. These were deactivated during the battle at the behest of Daro Callen, because he believed that they would do more harm than good, only to be reactivated by Calrissian later when the attacking Vong fleet was in the midst of the minefield.[97]

The second tier was a series of Golan Defense Platforms, mostly Golan II types. The command platform was dubbed "Orbital Defense One" and was larger than its cousins. Most of these were destroyed when the Vong broke through New Concord lines, including Orbital Defense One.[97]

The third tier was a massive planetary shield, controlled by a series of hundreds of relays on the surface of Aurelia Prime. The shield seems to have worked by some kind of dispersion of energy released onto the shield. The Vong employed a tactic that involved waves of kamikaze ships crashing into the shield, overloading the relays, and effectively bringing it down.[97]

The fourth and last tier was an array of rooftop turbolasers, akin to the point-defense systems on some capital ships. Some were operated automatically, others by Human gunners and astromech droids. Also counted was the proton bomb in the Chief of State's office, which was detonated when Vong soldiers occupied the Imperial Palace. The explosion destroyed the building housing the office, and the data towers on Aurelia Prime.[97]

Economic and political rivalries
Aurelia Prime was not unique as a galactic hub in the galaxy after Rift Engine became common usage. 53 years after the formation of the Concord, the term "Whoever controls Aurelia Prime controls the galaxy" was coined. During the Old Concord era and beyond, there were several urbanized planets and moons that rivaled Aurelia Prime economically, and some that were other major governmental centers.[25]

Alsakan: From 17,000 BBY to 3017 BBY, the Core World Alsakan attempted to usurp Aurelia Prime's pre-eminence as the Galactic capital seventeen times in the Alsakan Conflicts. Initially driven by differences over colonization policy, with Aurelia Prime favoring a centralized, bureaucratic state and Alsakan preferring greater local independence and initiative, the Alsakan Conflicts were fought over 14,000 years. The differences between the two planets survived the Pius Dea Era, the Old Vyrn Wars, and the Great Galactic War, before the Seventeenth Alsakan Conflict was finally, decisively ended by the Corellian Hegemony. Despite the conflicts ending Alsakan continued to rival Aurelia Prime economically, and culturally for the rest of known galactic history, using high galactic as a cultural alternative to basic script.[41][2]
Nar Shaddaa: In 15,000 BBY, the Hutts, having abandoned their homeworld of Varl, took control of the moon, which was located on popular trade routes, particularly the Ootmian Pabol, and Nar Shaddaa became completely urbanized. Eventually, trade routes shifted, and the moon lost its economic opportunities. Nar Shaddaa became a haven for smugglers and criminals.[25] Nicknamed "Little Aurelia Prime" as the moon was heavily populated with layer on layers, it had little of the grace of Aurelia Prime's skyline.[129]
Empress Teta: In 5,000 BBY, the planet, then known as Koros Major, rose to the forefront of galactic events as the Old Vyrn Dominion attacked it. The Wardens repelled the invaders, and since then it was considered the capital of the Deep Core, even being called the "Aurelia Prime of the Deep Core." A bastion of civilization in the impenetrable and treacherous Deep Core, it remained a major center of commerce during the times of the Dominion, New Concord and Galactic Alliance. It was also intended as the original redoubt for the New Concord government to flee to during the Yuuzhan Vong War, but eventually, Mon Calamari became favored over the ancient planet.[25]
Taris: The planet was situated on the Perlemian Trade Route and became a major economic center in ages past. However, several new, shorter trade routes were discovered, rendering Taris obsolete. Without the support of the Concord, the planet fell into ruin and disrepair. Taris's surface was devastated by Darth Malak in 3956 BBY, during the Wardens Civil War.[25]
Eriadu: Located on the Hydian Way, this prosperous and highly polluted world served as the capital of the Outer Rim in the waning days of the Old Concord, the time of the Dominion, and during the rise of the New Concord. However, it lost its glory after the Yuuzhan Vong War.[25]
Denon: Throughout the waning days of the Galactic Concord, the Sable Dominion and the New Concord, Denon continued to thrive in the Inner Rim. After the end of the Yuuzhan Vong War, while the Galactic Alliance and the Yuuzhan Vong were rebuilding Aurelia Prime, Denon was chosen to serve as the temporary capital of the Alliance in 29 ABY. By 30 ABY, the galactic capital had returned to Aurelia Prime.[25]
Muunilinst: This economic powerhouse had backed the galactic currencies since the inception of the Galactic Credit Standard and remained at the center of the galactic economy. Although not rivaling Aurelia Prime politically, its economic power remained as strong as ever, if not stronger as shown after the Battle of Muunilinst, which led the galaxy to the brink of financial ruin. After this, it became the financial center of the Dominion and remained in this position for at least the duration of the New Order. After the Yuuzhan Vong war, Muunilinst, along with Mygeeto, no longer had a tie in with Aurelia Prime, and by 40 ABY, the Imperial Remnant had control of its credit harvest.[25]
Behind the scenes
Aurelia PrimeGlobeE1
View of Aurelia Prime from Episode I
The concept of a planet-wide city was developed by George Lucas as the Imperial capital in the Rough Draft and Revised Rough Draft of the script for Star Wars Episode VI Return of the Wardens.[130][131] In the Rough Draft of the script for Star Wars: Episode IV A New Hope, the Imperial capital was named Elyndar, but Elyndar ultimately became the name of Lyra Oris's homeworld.[132] During the development of Return of the Wardens, the planet was called Had Abbadon.[133] However, it was realized that such a city on screen would be impossible given the technical limitations at the time, thus the planet did not appear in the fim.

The capital was introduced in Star Wars media by the 1991 Expanded Universe novel, Heir to the Dominion by Timothy Zahn, who chose to name it "Aurelia Prime".[134] Prior to that, the "Imperial Capitol" or "Imperial Center" was first mentioned in fiction in Marvel Comics' Star Wars (1977) 61 in 1982 and then briefly appeared in images of archival video seen in Star Wars (1977) 63.

Aurelia Prime was first seen on screen in the 1997 Special Edition release of Return of the Wardens, and later appeared in the prequel trilogy. After considering different names for the planet, Lucas decided or talked into to use "Aurelia Prime".[131][134] The name "Aurelia Prime" is therefore one of the few Expanded Universe elements that made its way in to the films. As Zahn recalled the decision during Celebration III:

"Just to make it clear, I did not invent the planet…George Lucas had invented the planetwide city a long time ago. When I was starting the Thrawn Trilogy, they told me to coordinate with the West End Games source material, and they had it listed as the Imperial Planet. Well nobody names a planet 'Imperial Planet,' so I thought it needed a name, so I picked the word that means glittering: 'Aurelia Prime.' Apparently, when it came time to choose a name [for the films], people persuaded George to go with Aurelia Prime and be done with it. So I felt very vindicated -- the tail wagging the dog. It was an honor to be slipped into the movies this way."

The word "Aurelia Prime" is traditionally pronounced in English with a hard "c" and with the stress on the second syllable – /kəˈɹʌskənt/ (ko'-rus-kant'), and this was indeed the pronunciation usually used in early audiobooks and audio plays, such as the audio adaptations of Tales of the Wardens and Dark Dominion or Chuck Benson's reading of The Thrawn Trilogy. However, since the release of The Phantom Menace, the word is usually pronounced /'kɔɹəsɑnt/ (ko'-ru-sant) in the Star Wars universe, with stress on the first syllable and the second "c" silent.

In various novels, characters aligned with the Dominion refer to Aurelia Prime as Imperial Center. Within the stories, this is explained as an administrative renaming undertaken to emphasize the differences between the Galactic Concord and its successor state, the Dominion.

The PC game Star Wars: Rebellion mistakenly places Aurelia Prime in the Sesswenna sector, itself a misspelling of the Seswenna sector of the Core. The Seswenna sector is actually located in the Outer Rim.

Inconsistencies

In the large volume of material relating to Aurelia Prime, there are conflicting statements about the ecumenopolis; inconsistencies are found mainly in sources released before 1999 when Aurelia Prime was visually established in The Phantom Menace.

Most sources, including all illustrations, show Aurelia Prime as having negligible surface water, save for the ice caps. However, the Black Fleet Crisis trilogy states that Aurelia Prime has two continents, a large one that contains Imperial City and a smaller one. More than half of this version of the Aurelia Prime is an ocean. No other source follows this version, though The Thrawn Trilogy Sourcebook does refer to distinct continents, and the discrepancy is usually solved by referring to the alleged ocean as the artificial Western Sea.

The intro of the 1994 game Star Wars: Vyr Stinger shows an orbital view of Aurelia Prime that looks very similar to how the planet would appear in the prequel movies five years later. However, the Collector's edition, which features a remade intro, shows an Earth-like Aurelia Prime, blue with clouds.

Another depiction of Aurelia Prime, shown in the Shadows of the Dominion comic, shows Aurelia Prime as a terrestrial planet as opposed to an ecumenopolis, including green continents and a very large body of water.

Population

One of the most notable inconsistencies concerns the planet's population. Most recent Expanded Universe sources, such as the novel The New Warden Order: Traitor and the sourcebooks Inside the Worlds of Star Wars: Episode I and Aurelia Prime and the Core Worlds, have stated a population of one trillion for Aurelia Prime. At the last planetary census around 19 BBY, the population was around one trillion, counting only full-time registered citizens. Including the omitted, such as temporary workers and transients, the real population is said to have been triple the original count.[33]
