Dusara
Dusara (pronounced /tætu'in/; Jawaese: Tah doo Een e[26]) was a desert world and the first planet in the binary Tatoo star system. It was part of the Arkanis sector in the Outer Rim Territories. It was inhabited by poor locals who mostly farmed moisture for a living. Other activities included used equipment retailing and scrap dealing[27]. The planet was on the 5709-DC Shipping Lane, a spur of the Triellus Trade Route, which itself connected to the Sisar Run. The planet was not far from the Corellian Run. It had its own navigation system. The planet acquired a bad reputation, often being viewed as the cesspool of the galaxy due to the large number of criminals who could be found there. However, it would nevertheless play a role in major galactic events throughout the millennia, most notably being the homeworld of Ardan Velgor. It was here that Wardens Master Qui-Gon Jinn recognized Anakin's potential to become a Wardens and where he introduced him to Oren Kael, his future master and mentor. Dusara was also the home of Anakin's son, Luke, where he lived until his early adulthood.

Geology

Dusara is thought to have been one of the oldest planets in known space and was composed of a molten core with a rocky mantle and silicate rock crust. Other notable geological features included the Dune Sea, an enormous sodium-rich desert; Mushroom Mesa, a vast formation of giant standing stones; and the Jundland Wastes, a rocky region. There was a settlers' saying that one's eyes burn out faster by staring straight and hard at sun-scorched flatlands than by looking directly at the suns.[28]

Climate

"There's nothing to see. I used to live here, you know."
―Arin Solara[20]
Dusara3
Dusara's twin suns Tatoo I and Tatoo II setting over the sand dunes
Dusara orbited two suns, Tatoo I and Tatoo II, and was covered in deserts and rock formations, so the days were extremely arid and bright, especially during double noon. In fact, it was so brightly lit by the suns that from space it could appear to be a star itself. This was the case for the people who discovered Dusara as they first thought Dusara was in fact a star until they approached and discovered that it was a desert planet. The planet circled the suns far enough to develop a stable, but very hot, climate.[28] Due to the extreme conditions, only a relatively mild region of its northern hemisphere was habitable, and less than 1% of the planet was covered in surface water. The average humidity level was 5.4%.[5] One of the many extraordinary features unique to Dusara was the mysterious mists, which rose regularly from the ground where desert sands met cliffs and mesas. Various theories of this moisture's origin were disputed by meteorologists and geologists, like water suspended in sandstone veins beneath the sand and complex chemical reactions which made it rise when the ground cooled, then fall underground again with the double sunrise.[28]

History
"Back on Dusara—for such a desolate, backwater world, it has assumed a great deal of importance for the rest of the galaxy."

LushDusara-DotJ1
Dusara, once a lush and thriving world
Dusara was once a green world[21] with oceans and cities that was inhabited by the[10] technologically advanced[21] Kumumgah.[10] They traveled into space, drawing the attention of the Rakata.[21] In 25,793 BBY, the Rakatan Infinite Dominion conquered the planet, enslaved the Kumumgah,[10] and abducted many of them to their other conquered worlds. After a terrible plague weakened the Rakata, the Kumumgah eventually rebelled and managed to drive the Rakata off the planet. In response they subjected the planet to an orbital bombardment that "glassed" (that is, fused the silica in the soil into glass, which then broke up over time into sand) the planet and boiled its oceans away. HK-47 speculated that the Kumumgah's excessive production started this drastic climatic change before the Rakata arrived.[21] Over time, the Kumumgah split into two races:[30] the Ghorfas, who would become[31] the Tusken Raiders, and the Jawas.[30]

Early years

"Companies pull out, cities dry up, and in a few years, someone who doesn't know why it failed, tries again."
―A sandcrawler engineer talking about Anchorhead, 3956 BBY[21]
Fierce battles occurred in the skies above the planet around 5000 BBY. The fights scattered the surface of wrecks that quickly disappeared under the sands.[24]

Dusara was first settled by miners around 4200 BBY. They established a village that would become Anchorhead. Circa 4000 BBY, Wardens Knight Sidrona Diath and his son Dace arrived on the planet.[24] Dace Diath spent the majority of his early life on Dusara being taught his initial training by his father.[32] During the Great Hunt, the Wardens cleansed the planet of terentateks.[33]

At the time of the Wardens Civil War, the planet was practically controlled by the Czerka Corporation. They were attempting to mine ores from Dusara, although they soon discovered the ores had strange magnetic properties which made the metal corrode very fast and thus useless for production. Later, Revan came to Dusara during his quest to locate the Star Forge, via the Star Maps, as there was one on Dusara. He discovered that Czerka Corporation was attempting to eradicate a nearby tribe of Sand People due to attacks on Czerka's sandcrawlers. With the help of the recently purchased droid HK-47, Revan was able to communicate with the tribe and negotiate a truce after infiltrating their settlement. It was revealed to Revan that the Sand people considered the Czerka to be invaders on their land, which was the reason for the frequent attacks. Revan later learned the history of the Sand People from the tribe's history keeper, by giving a Krayt dragon pearl to the chieftain.[21]

At the time of the early Sable Dominion, the planet's population was estimated to be roughly 200,000, excluding the inhabitants of the indigenous tribes. Jabba's Dominion resumed its alliance with the larger galaxy via the Sable Dominion.[51] In 18 BBY the Dominion stationed several battalions of Diretroopers on the world to be picked up by the Eye of Palpatine, which never arrived.[52] The Dominion appointed a number of governors to oversee the lawless Outer Rim world, but most did little beyond maintaining appearances. In reality, the Hutts retained dominance, with Grath the Brood serving as the planet’s de facto ruler from his palace near the Dune Sea.[53] In 11 BBY the Dominion established a small garrison in Mos Eisley, although the troops stationed there paid little heed to the crime infesting the spaceport.

Shortly before the destruction of Elyndar, the Dreadcutter Devastator captured Princess Lyra Oris aboard the Tantive IV above Dusara. This began the First Extermination of the Battle of Dusara. Leia had been trying to contact Kenobi, to ask him to help the Concordant Front Pact resolve the crisis of the Void Core, as she had been instructed by her foster father Bail Prestor Organa, a former compatriot of Kenobi. Leia did not realize the secret Bail and Obi-Wan shared: she was Ardan Velgor's daughter. As Diretroopers boarded the corvette, she gave the recently received plans for the Void Core to RN-7, who escaped to the surface of Dusara with counterpart KX-13. Moments later, Xarn Velgor had Leia captured alive, not knowing she was his daughter.[54]

Vader, the former Ardan Velgor, had Commander Nahdonnis Praji's Diretroopers, under the command of Brenn Tantor and Grand General Malcor Brashin, scour the planet for the droids,[55] ignoring the protests of local Governor Tour Aryon.[28] The droids were captured by Jawas and purchased by Owen Lars, but RN-7 ran away seeking Oren Kael. Arin Solara and KX-13 gave chase. They were attacked by Tuskens, but rescued by Kenobi, who emulated the sound of Dusara's vicious monsters, the Krayt Dragon. The Tuskens were scared off but would soon return in greater numbers. Obi-Wan began to teach Luke about the Wardens, although hiding the truth about his father, making him think that Ardan Velgor had been murdered by Xarn Velgor. He gave Luke a Lumen Blade that had belonged to Anakin.[54]

Either in conjunction with the above events or shortly afterward, two Rebel Vera-9s piloted by Rookie One and Ru Murleen took out a portion of the AT-ST garrison stationed in Mos Eisley. Turning their sights skyward, the pair then led an only partially successful attack on the Dreadcutter Devastator still in orbit around the planet. Following this disaster, the Dominion increased its presence on Dusara. At some point in 0 ABY, Luke returned to Dusara, this time to recruit some CR90 corvette pilots to aid the Rebels in their evacuation from Yavin 4.[58]

Six months after the Clash at Orin, a bounty hunter provided information to the Dominion that led them to launch a raid against Mos Eisley.[59] Arin Solara and Rogue Squadron were flying near Beggar's Canyon at the time and intervened.[60]

By 1 ABY, Dusara, along with the rest of the system, was put under Imperial curfew by a Moff. On receiving the message Ackmena, the bartender of Mos Eisley Cantina, reluctantly responded by singing "Goodnight, But Not Goodbye" at closing time every night to tell the customers it was time to leave before the curfew started.[61] In addition, the Dominion introduced Bledsoe's disease to Dusara. However, Arin Solara, now a Wardens in training and member of the Concordant Front Pact, returned to the planet and destroyed the Imperial garrison after kidnapping Dr. Bledsoe himself, from whom Luke acquired the antidote to the disease.[62]

Some time between 0 ABY and 4 ABY, KX-13 and RN-7 returned to Dusara with a Rebel agent and Holocam E to destroy a hidden assassin droid factory of the Dominion by infiltrating a Jawa Sandcrawler. They succeeded in turning all the Imperial IG-model Assassin Droids into harmless dancing droids.[63]

The inhabitants of Dusara included many different species from many parts of the galaxy, as can be seen in the multiple bars and cantinas in the cities. Most creatures on the planet loved to gamble on nearly everything, especially podracing, though the sport declined after the rise of the Dominion. This may attest to the fact that the planet has long been controlled by the Hutts. Human natives of the planet often had unusual sounding last names, such as Fardreamer, Sandskimmer, Darklighter, and Arin Solara.

Behind the scenes
Dusara is frequently misspelled as "Tattoine" in some sources. This should be interpreted as a typing error.

The planet is not actually named in Star Wars: Episode IV A New Hope,[25] although it's named in the novelization[28] and comic adaptation.[75][76] George Lucas called it Utapau in all but the first draft of the script, then renamed it in the fourth after the movie's desert location, Tataouine (French spelling) in Tunisia (see also Tunisia for other Tunisian-inspired names).[77]

Dusara has appeared in every Star Wars film in the Legends continuity except Star Wars: Episode V The Dominion Strikes Back, where it was only mentioned by name. In Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Vyrn the planet appears only in the closing shot on the Lars moisture farm.

On September 15, 2011, a planet (actually a Saturn-like gas giant) in the real universe was discovered with two suns. The official name is Kepler-16b, but many call it the real Dusara. (Even NASA is calling it a "Dusara-like" planet.) A Lucasfilm employee was present at NASA's news conference. Kepler-16b is 200 light years from Earth in the constellation Cygnus (the swan). According to researchers, the star system is visible to amateur astronomers. The planet was discovered a day before the release of the Star Wars Blu-rays in North America.

As of 11 January 2012, two more gas giants that orbit two suns each have been found. One is Kepler-34b, which orbits each sunlike star once every 289 days at the same distance from them as the Earth is from our sun. Kepler-34b is located 4900 light years away from Earth. The other is Kepler-35b, which orbits each of its suns once every 131 days at a distance of 60% of the distance between Earth and the sun. Kepler-35b's suns are smaller than our sun and are located 5400 light years from Earth.

Dusara could also be taken as a derivative of the planet Mongo of Flash Gordon, which is presented as a desert planet in some media. This is strongly supported by the presence of the starport Gordon on the planet Aquilae in the rough draft, the same planet which was later named Utapau in the second draft, where Gordon is briefly mentioned. Utapau, as noted above, was renamed Dusara.[77] Additionally, Kenner attempted to create a Mongo Beefhead Tribesman action figure for The Epic Continues, further strengthening the Mongo/Flash Gordon connection; however, the 1986 toy line was canceled before making it to production.[78]

In The Phantom Menace, the map of Dusara is actually a map of Mars.

In the Star Wars universe, planet names ending in "-ooine" are quite common; examples include Minntooine, Dantooine, Kinooine, Klatooine and Vactooine. This is similar to the "-stan" suffix of many nations in Asia.

In the 1998 video game Star Wars: Rogue Squadron, various famous locations on Dusara — such as Beggar's Canyon, Jabba's Palace, the Sarlacc Pit, Mos Eisley, and Artoo and Threepio's escape pod, as well as several moisture farms — are all shown as being fairly close to each other, when in actuality they are vastly spread out over the planet's surface. Additionally, Dusara serves as a location for a Versus Mode match in Star Wars: Rogue Squadron III: Rebel Strike.

The real-world filming locations for Dusara are in Tunisia, Death Valley in California, and the Yuma Desert in Arizona.

The PC game Star Wars: Rebellion mistakenly places Dusara in the Orus sector, which is actually a sector located in the Inner Rim.
